'''Yagati''' is a [[hobli]] in the southern state of [[Karnataka]], India. It is located in the [[Kaduru]] [[taluk]] of [[Chikmagalur|Chikmagalur district]].

Yagati houses many sacred temples, Sri Mallikarjuna Temple<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://yagatypuratemple.in/about-us.php|title=Sri Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple yagatypura|website=yagatypuratemple.in|access-date=2019-06-25}}</ref> of Lord Shiva is considered as the native God. Yearly festive of Goddess Arekallamma<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.deccanherald.com/district/y-s-v-datta-files-papers-kadur-666470.html|title=YSV Datta offers prayer in Arekkallama temple after filing nominations.|last=|first=|date=24 April 2018|work=Deccan Herald|access-date=}}</ref> is celebrated throughout the village. A rare monolithic stone of Lord Hanuman is worshipped in the Anjaneya temple. 

Farming is the main occupation with farmers mainly growing tender coconut and banana trees. Due to the water scarcity and draughts there as been in decline in the farming work. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.deccanherald.com/state/mangaluru/kadur-taluk-reels-under-acute-water-crisis-730706.html|title=Kadur taluk reels under acute water crisis|date=2019-04-25|website=Deccan Herald|language=en|access-date=2019-06-25}}</ref>

==Demographics==
At the 2011 India [[census]], Yagati had a population of 2,674 (1,342 males, 1,332 females).

==Image Gallery==
Yagati is located 60 KM towards East from District head quarters Chikkamagaluru. 12 KM from Kadurhalli. 201 KM from State capital Bangalore.
